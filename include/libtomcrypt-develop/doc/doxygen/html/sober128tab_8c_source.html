89 /*
90  * This is really the combination of two SBoxes; the least significant
91  * 24 bits comes from:
92  * 8->32 Sbox generated by Millan et. al. at Queensland University of
93  * Technology. See: E. Dawson, W. Millan, L. Burnett, G. Carter,
94  * "On the Design of 8*32 S-boxes". Unpublished report, by the
95  * Information Systems Research Centre,
96  * Queensland University of Technology, 1999.
97  *
98  * The most significant 8 bits are the Skipjack "F table", which can be
99  * found at http://csrc.nist.gov/CryptoToolkit/skipjack/skipjack.pdf .
100  * In this optimised table, though, the intent is to XOR the word from
101  * the table selected by the high byte with the input word. Thus, the
102  * high byte is actually the Skipjack F-table entry XORED with its
103  * table index.
104  */
